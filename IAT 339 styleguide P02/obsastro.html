Observational Astronomy

from our moon to the entire observable universe

This class introduces Methods of observataional astronomy, stargazing, life-time of stars, stellar evolution, galaxy evolution and biggest cosmology questions

I: Our night sky and our solar system

Introduces methods of Observational Astronomy, staargazing and our neighbours within the Solar system with Newton's Laws of Motions and Kepler's Law

II: Stellar Evolution and the life time of a star

Topics include blackbody, blackbody radiation, main-sequence stars, red and blue giants, neutron stars, white dwarfs, super novae,the Hertzsprung-Russell Diagram.

III: Galaxies, Black holes

Introduces types of galaxies (sprial, barred-spiral, elliptical, lenticular), formation of blackholes,

IV: Cosmology and Big Bang

Topics include the origins and fate of universe, quantum fluctuation, cosmic inflation, big bang, dark matter , dark energy.
